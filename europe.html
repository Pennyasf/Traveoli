In Spain paella is still unique. Many Spanish families congregate on mass to eat paella in restaurants, and it is easy to see why - paella can create a party atmosphere and often a debate (often over the making of the paella itself!!). You want to impress your friends, "paella" is pronounced "pa-e-ya" with the "e" as in "bet".

Seafood Paella

INGREDIENTS

  • 20-24 raw shell-on king prawns
  • 500g monkfish, cut into chunks
    • li>1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 500g paella rice
    • li>4 garlic cloves, sliced
  • ½ x 400g can chopped tomatoes
    • li>500g mussels, cleaned
  • 100g frozen baby broad beans handful parsley leaves, roughly chopped

METHOD

  1. Peel and de-vein the prawns, reserving the heads and shells. Return the prawns to the fridge.
  2. To make the stock, heat the oil in a large pan over a medium-high heat and add the onion, tomatoes, garlic, and reserved prawn shells and heads. Cook for 3-4 mins, then pour in 2 litres of water and add the stock cube and star anise.
  3. Heat the oil in a large paella pan or an extra-large frying pan. Brown the monkfish for a few mins each side, then remove and set aside. Add the onion and fry for 4-5 mins until softened.
  4. Stir in the rice and cook for 30 secs to toast. Add the garlic, paprika, and saffron, cook for another 30 secs, then stir in the tomatoes and 1.5 litres of the fish stock.
  5. Return the monkfish to the pan with the prawns, mussels, peas and broad beans.
  6. Cover the pan with a large baking tray, or foil, and cook on a low heat for another 10-15 mins until the mussels are open and the prawns are cooked through.
  7. Scatter the parsley before serving.

Margherita Pizza

INGREDIENTS

  • 300g strong bread flour
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 100ml passata
  • handful fresh basil
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 125g ball mozzarella, sliced
  • handful grated or shaved parmesan

METHOD

  1. Make the base: Put the flour into a large bowl, then stir in the yeast and salt. Make a well, pour in 200ml warm water and the olive oil and bring together with a wooden spoon until you have a soft, fairly wet dough. Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5 mins until smooth.
  2. Make the sauce: Mix the passata, basil and crushed garlic together, then season to taste. Leave to stand at room temperature while you get on with shaping the base.
  3. Roll out the dough: If you’ve let the dough rise, give it a quick knead, then split into two balls. On a floured surface, roll out the dough into large rounds, about 25cm across, using a rolling pin.
  4. Top and bake: Heat oven to 240C/fan 220C /gas 8. Put another baking sheet or an upturned baking tray in the oven on the top shelf. Smooth sauce over bases with the back of a spoon. Scatter with cheese and tomatoes, drizzle with olive oil and season. Put one pizza, still on its baking sheet, on top of the preheated sheet or tray. Bake for 8-10 mins until crisp
